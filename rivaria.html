Games About Me Itch.io
Rivaria is a game I made in one month for a game jam supporting the Team Seas movement. The theme was water cleanup and I choose to make a game about teaming up with a group of volunteers to clean up the river and stop the trash being thrown in the river in the first place.

I wanted to use the gameplay to explore the actions needed to be taken in real life to help reverse water polution (albeit in a more gameified manner), with the hope of inspiring people to get involved with cleanup efforts.


In the beginning, the player-character gets annoyed with the trash washing up next to their house and begins cleaning as much of it as they can, but being unable to make a significant difference.  They share their cleanup effort online with their comunity and people begin volunteering to help with the cleanup.

The more people join, the more effective they become at cleaning the river and eventually gain enough support to help influence local policy in the city. They campaign to reduce the polution dumped into the water down to zero and help clean all of the trash remaining in the river.



The biggest design challenge with this project was balancing the economy. The economy can make or break a clicker/management game as it can easily become either way too grindy, or way too easy, and the line between the two is often very slim.

The game consists of multiple upgrade/ability systems that are slowly introduced to the player and build on top of each other.
